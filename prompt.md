Create a responsive landing page for a web app called “Misky”.

Context:
Misky is a free web app for Peruvian families that helps them decide what to cook every day.
It suggests daily meals using local, affordable ingredients, calculates portions for the whole family, and helps avoid overspending.
The tone must feel warm, human, and home-like — not institutional, not techy, not medical.

Target audience:
People who cook for their families (parents, caregivers, grandparents).
They are busy, budget-conscious, and tired of asking “What do we eat today?”.

Brand:
- Name: Misky
- Tagline: “¿Qué comemos hoy?”
- Brand values: warmth, simplicity, trust, everyday usefulness
- Cultural context: Peru (markets, family meals, home cooking)
- Avoid stereotypes or folkloric exaggeration

Design style:
- Warm, minimal, friendly
- Colors:
  - Primary: soft terracotta
  - Background: warm cream
  - Secondary: sage green
  - Text: dark warm gray
- Rounded corners, soft shadows
- Mobile-first but looks great on desktop

Page structure:

1. Hero section
- Big headline: “¿Qué comemos hoy?”
- Subheadline: “Misky te ayuda a planear la comida de tu familia, sin complicarte.”
- Primary CTA button: “Empezar gratis”
- Secondary text: “Gratis. Sin anuncios. Pensado para familias.”

2. Problem section
Short statements, very relatable:
- “Pensar qué cocinar todos los días cansa.”
- “El presupuesto no siempre alcanza.”
- “A veces hay comida, pero no ideas.”
Use icons or simple illustrations.

3. Solution section
Explain how Misky helps:
- Sugiere comidas diarias
- Calcula porciones según cuántos son
- Usa ingredientes comunes del mercado
- Ayuda a no gastar de más

4. How it works (3 steps)
- Configura tu familia
- Elige qué cocinar hoy
- Cocina tranquilo

5. Trust section
- Mention that recipes are based on validated nutritional sources (without heavy institutional logos)
- Tone: “Puedes confiar, está bien hecho.”

6. Final CTA
- Headline: “Hoy se come bien.”
- Button: “Probar Misky”

Copy tone:
- Friendly, simple, conversational
- No technical jargon
- Sounds like someone at home, not a nutritionist

Language:
Spanish (Peru)
